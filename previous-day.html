Is 60 not enough?



In the totality of basketball history, a remarkable and exclusive achievement stands out: only three games have featured a player from the losing team scoring more than 60 points, and all three instances bear the indelible mark of basketball legend Michael Jordan. His unparalleled scoring prowess manifested in these games, where he tallied 64 (01/16/1993 vs Orlando Magic), 63(04/20/1986 @ Boston Celtics, playoff scoring record to this day), and 61 (04/16/1987 vs Atlanta Hawks) points despite ultimately ending up on the losing side. This extraordinary feat underscores not only Jordan's individual brilliance but also the rarity of such exceptional performances in the realm of professional basketball.
